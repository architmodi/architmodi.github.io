Install firewall-‚Å£

Enable/ Disable/ Restart/ Check status-‚Å£

1. Enable firewall-‚Å£

2. Disable firewall-‚Å£

3. Restart firewall-‚Å£

View IP table-‚Å£

‚Å£

Change input policies (accept/reject/drop IP addresses). P.S. Users can change the input policies only by flushing the existing policies.‚Å£

1. Find the input policies with command-‚Å£

iptables -L | grep INPUT‚Å£

2. Flush the input policies with command-‚Å£

iptables -F‚Å£

3. Verify that the policies have been flushed by rechecking the IP list-‚Å£

iptables -L‚Å£

4. Change input policies to reject with-‚Å£

iptables -A INPUT ‚Äîprotocol icmp ‚Äîin-interface enp0s3 -j REJECT‚Å£

5. Verify if the reject policy has been applied to the icmp protocol-‚Å£

iptables -L | grep REJECT‚Å£

6. To change the policies to drop, flush the policies again with the command iptables -F and change to drop with-‚Å£

iptables -A INPUT ‚Äîprotocol icmp ‚Äîin-interface enp0s3 -j DROP‚Å£

7. Verify the change with-‚Å£

iptables -L | grep DROP‚Å£

‚Å£

Block TCP traffic-‚Å£

iptables -A INPUT -p tcp -s xxx.xxx.xxx.xxx -j DROP‚Å£

‚Å£

Block/unblock a particular IP address-‚Å£

1. Block-‚Å£

iptables -A INPUT -s xxx.xxx.xxx.xxx -j DROP‚Å£

2. Unblock-‚Å£

iptables -D INPUT -s xxx.xxx.xxx.xxx -j DROP‚Å£

Block outgoing connections on specific ports-‚Å£

iptables -A OUTPUT -p tcp –dport xxx -j DROP‚Å£

‚Å£

Block incoming connections on specific ports-‚Å£

iptables -A INPUT -p tcp –dport xxx -j ACCEPT‚Å£

‚Å£

Implement multiport access for incoming traffic-‚Å£

‚Å£

Implement multiport access for outgoing traffic-‚Å£

  1. How would you explain your stem field to young girls?

We are the teeth doctors! We specialise in oral health and diagnose oral diseases. We create treatment plans for patients and help maintain or restore their oral health, we also take x-rays, monitor growth and development of teeth/jaws, and perform surgeries if necessary. In short, we help you keep your mouth happy.

This was really important to me but at the same time I was running short on prep time. The pressure was real and unfortunately I got hit by anxiety. Long story short, I pushed through and got myself a nice seat for dentistry and I am enjoying this field ever since.

3. What are some really cool things that people in your profession work on?

What is usually the first thing you notice in a person? Smile. So I guess one of the coolest things that people in my profession do is smile designing. I strongly believe that one of the worst pains in human body is tooth ache, so it makes me happy to offer my help for relieving that pain. It warms my heart when I see a smile back on my patient‚Äôs faces. My work gives me tremendous satisfaction and my happy patients is what keeps me going.

5. What goals do you have for the future?

A lot of people even in the developed countries ignore dental health. I would really like to spread awareness about importance of dental hygiene and help avoid the risk of diseases like oral cancer.
